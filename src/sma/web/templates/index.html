price math · why curtail below 0.0153 €/kWh (= 15.31 €/MWh) click to expand · formulas + live substitution
ecopower formula
net injection (€/kWh) =
0.98 × EPEX − 0.015
EPEX = day-ahead spot price for BE, fixed per 15-min slot. The 0.98 covers Ecopower's market spread, the −0.015 is their fixed handling deduction.
break-even derivation
set injection = 0
0.98 · X − 0.015 = 0
0.98 · X         = 0.015
        X        = 0.015 / 0.98
        X        = 0.0153 €/kWh
                  (= 15.31 €/MWh)
Below this price, every kWh you push to the grid earns negative money. That's why 15 is the cutoff.

= 1.02 · EPEX + 0.1315
why both prices matter

Consumption is what the grid charges you when you import. It's almost flat (~0.1315 €/kWh baseline) because most of it is taxes and network fees, with a tiny EPEX-linked component.

The asymmetry between injection (≈ EPEX − 0.015) and consumption (≈ EPEX + 0.1315) is what powers the economic rail — when the home is consuming, even at deeply negative spot, self-consumption beats curtailment because we'd otherwise be importing at this price.
